The Watts Towers, built between 1921 and 1954, were not commissioned by any institution or individual, but were instead the passionate personal project of Italian immigrant Simon Rodia (not Roda). Located in the Watts neighborhood of Los Angeles, California, at approximately 33.9°N latitude and 118.2°W longitude, this extraordinary collection of 17 interconnected sculptural structures stands as a pivotal example of arts and culture in Los Angeles. Rising dramatically from the urban landscape, these towers—reaching heights of up to 99 feet—represent one of America's most remarkable examples of outsider art and architectural folk art. Rodia, working alone with simple hand tools and found materials including steel rebar, concrete, and decorative elements like broken glass, seashells, and pottery fragments, created this visionary environment that has since become a National Historic Landmark and continues to inspire artists and visitors alike with its testament to human creativity, perseverance, and imagination.
